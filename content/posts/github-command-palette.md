I switched jobs recently and went from working mostly in a self hosted Gitlab instance to Github. Going beyond my mostly personal, isolated repos in Github really made me miss GitLab's command palette for navigating within organizations with multiple repos. But it turns out [Github has something similar,](https://docs.github.com/en/get-started/accessibility/github-command-palette), hidden behind a feature preview toggle. Here's how to enable it:

* Click your profile pic in the upper right corner.
* Select `Feature Preview`
* Turn on `Command Palette`

Then you can use `ctrl`/`cmd` + `k` to bring up the palette for quick navigation. Easily jump to sections within the current repo (actions, pull requests, issues etc) or to other repos in your org (or ones you recently browsed). Dozens of seconds saved every day!

p.s. turns out this thing has a [longer](https://www.theregister.com/2025/07/22/github_command_palette_backtrack/) and a somewhat [controversial history](https://github.blog/changelog/2025-07-16-upcoming-deprecation-of-github-command-palette-feature-preview/)
